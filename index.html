The Context

Why These Workshops Are Needed

Young people today face growing pressure in many areas of life. Academic demands, uncertainty about the future, social expectations, and constant exposure to digital media shape much of their daily experience.

"The ability to express oneself is not a luxury. It is a fundamental part of human development."

Many young people move between environments that ask them to perform, achieve, or adapt. Few spaces invite them to slow down and explore their thoughts, emotions, and ideas without judgment. As a result, opportunities for genuine self-expression are often limited.

At the same time, meaningful human connection has become increasingly difficult to find. Interaction is often brief, transactional, or mediated through screens. Many young people lack spaces where they can feel seen, heard, and accepted for who they are. This challenge extends beyond youth—parents seek deeper connections, older adults face isolation, and communities lack environments that bring people together.

Founders

Who We Are

We are two young women who share a belief in the power of creativity to bring people closer to themselves, to one another, and to their communities.

Co-Founder

Ann Antwan

Dentist & Community Development Specialist

Ann has spent the past five years working with humanitarian organizations across Iraq, focusing on youth development, community engagement, and leadership. Throughout her career, she has led programs that create platforms for young people to express their ideas and take an active role in their communities.

Co-Founder

Tumha Dawod

Clinical Psychology Graduate & MHPSS Manager

Tumha brings more than seven years of experience designing and managing mental health and psychosocial support (MHPSS) programs across Iraq. Her work focuses on supporting individuals, families, and communities to strengthen well-being and build resilience.
